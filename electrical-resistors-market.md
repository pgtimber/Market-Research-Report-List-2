<p><ul><li>Wire Wound Resistor</li><li>Carbon Film Resistor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Electrical resistors are commonly categorized into two main types: wire wound resistors and carbon film resistors. Wire wound resistors are made by winding a resistance wire around an insulating core, offering high stability and accuracy in resistance values. On the other hand, carbon film resistors use a thin layer of carbon film on a ceramic rod, providing good precision and reliability. Both types are widely used in various electronic applications for controlling the flow of current and voltage in circuits.</p></p>
