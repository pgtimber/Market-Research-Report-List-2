<p><p>Edible oil co-products are divided into two main categories: liquid co-products and solid co-products. Liquid co-products include items like oil sludge, soapstock, and fatty acids, which are typically used in the production of soaps, detergents, and animal feeds. Solid co-products, on the other hand, consist of items like oil cakes and deoiled meals, which are commonly used as livestock feed. Both types of co-products play a significant role in the edible oil industry by maximizing the utilization of raw materials and minimizing waste.</p></p>
