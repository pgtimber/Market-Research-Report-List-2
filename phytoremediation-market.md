<p><p>Phytoremediation is a process in which plants are used to clean up contaminated soil, air, and water. There are six main types of phytoremediation markets: Phytosequestration involves storing contaminants in plant tissues; Rhizodegradation uses plant roots to break down contaminants in the soil; Phytohydraulics uses plants to clean up water through uptake and transpiration; Phytoextraction involves plants absorbing contaminants from the soil; Phytovolatilization releases contaminants into the air through plant leaves; and Phytodegradation uses plants to break down contaminants directly.</p></p>
