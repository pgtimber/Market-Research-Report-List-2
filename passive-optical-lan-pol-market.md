<p><p>Passive Optical LAN (POL) refers to a network technology that uses fiber optic cables to deliver data instead of traditional copper wires. There are two main market types within the POL market, namely GPON (Gigabit Passive Optical Network) and EPON (Ethernet Passive Optical Network). GPON offers higher data speeds and is commonly used for larger enterprises, while EPON is more cost-effective and suitable for smaller businesses. Both technologies provide reliable and efficient connectivity, making them popular choices in the network infrastructure market.</p></p>
