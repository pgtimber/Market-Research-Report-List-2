<p><p>Antimicrobial food packaging additives are substances added to packaging materials to prevent the growth of harmful microorganisms and increase the shelf life of food products. Types of additives include organic acids, enzymes, fungicides, essential oils, and others. Organic acids such as citric and lactic acid are commonly used for their antimicrobial properties. Enzymes work by breaking down harmful microorganisms. Fungicides target fungi that can spoil food. Essential oils, like oregano and thyme oil, have antimicrobial properties. Other additives may include antimicrobial peptides or bacteriophages.</p></p>
