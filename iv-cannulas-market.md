<p><p>IV Cannulas are medical devices used to administer fluids, medication, or blood products directly into a patient's bloodstream. The market for IV Cannulas is divided into three main types: Peripheral IV Cannula, which is inserted into a peripheral vein in the arms, hands, or legs; Central Line IV Cannula, which is inserted into a major vein near the heart; and Midline IV Cannula, which is longer than a peripheral IV but shorter than a central line, and is typically inserted in the upper arm. Each type of IV Cannula serves a specific medical purpose and is used based on the patient's needs.</p></p>
