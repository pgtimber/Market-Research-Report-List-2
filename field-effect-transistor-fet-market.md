<p><p>Field Effect Transistors (FETs) are semiconductor devices used for amplifying or switching electronic signals. There are two main types of FETs: Junction Field Effect Transistors (JFET) and Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor Field-Effect Transistors (MOSFET). JFETs operate by controlling the current between two terminals using a voltage applied to a semiconductor material. MOSFETs use an insulated gate to control the flow of current between two terminals, making them more efficient and commonly used in digital circuits. These two types of FETs dominate the market for semiconductor devices.</p></p>
