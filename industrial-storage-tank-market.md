<p><p>Industrial storage tanks can be categorized into two main types: pressurized and non-pressurized tanks. Pressurized tanks are designed to store materials under pressure, maintaining a constant pressure inside the tank. They are commonly used for storing gases or liquids that require pressure control. On the other hand, non-pressurized tanks are not designed to hold pressure and are typically used for storing materials that do not require pressurization, such as water or chemicals. Both types of tanks play a crucial role in a wide range of industries for storage purposes.</p></p>
